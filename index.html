Sentiment in Mind: Evaluating Zero-Shot and Fine-Tuned Open-Source Large Language Models for Multi-Class Mental Health Text Classification
Manuscript submitted to Big Data Research (Elsevier) â€“ Under Review

Sentiment in Mind is a research project where I compare multiple open-source large language models (DistilBERT, DeepSeek, Mistral-7B, Gemma, and Phi-3.5) for multi-class mental health text classification. I build a full NLP pipeline using Hugging Face and LoRA-based 4-bit fine-tuning on a combined dataset of 13k+ real-world mental-health posts (anxiety, depression, stress, suicidal ideation, etc.). The work evaluates both zero-shot and fine-tuned models using accuracy, macro-F1, and detailed error analysis, with a focus on reliability and safe use of LLMs in mental-health support scenarios. The manuscript is currently under review at Big Data Research (Elsevier).
